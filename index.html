Biography

APJ Abdul Kalam, full name Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, was an eminent Indian scientist and politician who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India, and passed away on July 27, 2015. Kalam was a highly respected aerospace engineer and played a significant role in India's space and missile development programs. He was a key figure in the development of the country's first indigenous satellite launch vehicle, SLV-III, and the Pokhran-II nuclear tests. For his contributions to India's defense and space research, he became known as the "Missile Man of India." Aside from his scientific achievements, Kalam was also renowned for his humility, simplicity, and dedication to inspiring the youth of the nation. He often interacted with students and urged them to pursue their dreams and contribute to the development of India. After serving as the President of India from 2002 to 2007, Kalam continued to be actively involved in various educational and societal initiatives. He was a prolific writer and authored several books, including his autobiography "Wings of Fire." His work and life story continue to serve as a source of inspiration for millions of people in India and around the world. APJ Abdul Kalam's passing in 2015 was mourned by the entire nation, and his legacy lives on as a symbol of dedication, hard work, and the pursuit of knowledge.

Books written by Dr. Kalam

APJ Abdul Kalam was a prolific writer, and he authored several books on a wide range of topics, including science, education, leadership, and motivation. Some of his most notable books include:

"Wings of Fire: An Autobiography":

This book is Kalam's autobiography and narrates his early life, career as a scientist, and his journey to becoming the President of India. It offers insights into his personal and professional experiences.

"Ignited Minds: Unleashing the Power Within India":

In this book, Kalam shares his vision for India's development and calls upon the youth to take charge of the country's future by channeling their potential and creativity.

"India 2020: A Vision for the New Millennium":

Co-authored with Y.S. Rajan, this book presents a comprehensive vision for India's growth and development by the year 2020, with a focus on technology and socio-economic changes.

"The Scientific Indian: A Twenty-First Century Guide to the World Around Us":

This book explores various scientific concepts in a simple and engaging manner, aimed at making science accessible to the common man.

"Envisioning an Empowered Nation: Technology for Societal Transformation":

Co-authored with A. Sivathanu Pillai, this book discusses the potential of technology in transforming the lives of people in India.

"Transcendence: My Spiritual Experiences with Pramukh Swamiji":

Kalam shares his experiences and interactions with Pramukh Swamiji, the spiritual leader of the BAPS Swaminarayan Sanstha.

"Guiding Souls: Dialogues on the Purpose of Life":

In this book, Kalam engages in a series of conversations with people from various walks of life to explore the purpose and meaning of life.

"Target 3 Billion":

Co-authored with Srijan Pal Singh, this book discusses a sustainable and comprehensive plan to address the challenges faced by the world's poorest three billion people.

These are just a few of the many books authored by APJ Abdul Kalam. His writings reflect his deep wisdom, passion for education, and commitment to the betterment of society. His books continue to inspire and motivate people across the globe.

Timeline

Here is a timeline of major events in the life of APJ Abdul Kalam:

  • October 15, 1931: Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam is born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India.
  • 1954: Kalam graduates in Physics from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli.
  • 1955-1960: He joins the Madras Institute of Technology (MIT) and completes his Aeronautical Engineering degree.
  • 1960-1982: Kalam starts his career at the Aeronautical Development Establishment (ADE) as a scientist and later moves to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO).
  • 1969: Kalam plays a key role as the Project Director for India's first indigenous Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III) program, which successfully launches the Rohini satellite into orbit.
  • 1982-1992: He serves as the Director of the Defence Research and Development Laboratory (DRDL) and oversees various missile development programs.
  • 1992-1999: Kalam becomes the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister of India and the Secretary of the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO).
  • May 11, 1998: Kalam is associated with the successful nuclear tests conducted at Pokhran, which earns him the moniker "Missile Man of India."
  • July 25, 2002: APJ Abdul Kalam is elected as the 11th President of India, becoming the first scientist and bachelor to hold this esteemed position.
  • July 25, 2002 - July 25, 2007: Kalam serves as the President of India, during which he promotes various initiatives related to education, rural development, and youth empowerment.
  • 2010: He launches the "What Can I Give Movement," urging citizens to contribute to societal development in their own capacity.

Awards of Kalam

  • Padma Bhushan (1981): Kalam was awarded the Padma Bhushan, the third-highest civilian award in India, for his contributions to the Indian space program.
  • Padma Vibhushan (1990): India's second-highest civilian award for exceptional and distinguished service.
  • Bharat Ratna (1997): India's highest civilian award, presented in recognition of exceptional service or performance of the highest order without distinction of race, occupation, position, or sex.
  • Veer Savarkar Award (1998): Awarded by the Government of India in honor of Vinayak Damodar Savarkar, an independence activist and social reformer.
  • Ramanujan Award (2000): Presented by the Alwars Research Centre, Chennai, for his lifetime achievements in promoting the scientific growth of India.
  • Indira Gandhi Award for National Integration (2007): Given by the Indian National Congress party for his efforts in promoting national integration.
  • Hoover Medal (2009): Presented by the ASME Foundation of the United States for outstanding public service by an engineer.
  • King Charles II Medal (2007): Conferred by the Royal Society, UK, for his outstanding contribution to science.
